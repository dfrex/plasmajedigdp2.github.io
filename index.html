Overview

    We are Plasma JEDI, a group of masters students studying aeronautics and astronautics engineering at the University of Southampton.

    We are designing and building a handheld non-thermal plasma jet emitter for sterilisation purposes on space missions. Various scientific research has shown that non-thermal plasma can be used to decompose toxic chemicals, kill cancer cells and sterilise wounds, among other medical applications.

    After the successful test of a prototype device last year, the second year of this project aims to improve the design of the device and demonstrate its safety. This would represent a huge step towards the viable use of such a device in a spaceship environment.

    The goal for this year of the project is to be able to generate a cold plasma jet that is at least 5cm long, from a device that is about the same size as a lightsaber.

    Measuring Success

    Over the course of our degrees, we have build up the skills and knowledge required to design, manufacture and test a device such as this, as well as simulate the principle mechanics behind its operation and measure how well it performs compared to current plasma jet equipment.

    Studying in Southampton gives us the great opportunity to test our device in the university's recently refurbished propulsion lab. With the help of available image spectroscopy and slow motion cameras, we will be able to analyse the composition and flow of the jet to determine performance characteristics and areas to improve on the design.

    We are also taking advantage of the close proximity we have to Southampton General Hospital, who have the facilities to test samples of material that have been exposed to the jet, giving us a unique insight into how different tissues react to cold plasma.
